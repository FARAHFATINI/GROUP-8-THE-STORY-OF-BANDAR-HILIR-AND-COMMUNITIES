Historical Origins





History of Bandar Hilir

Basically, in the late of 14th century, in the Era of Parameswara, who was the prince from nearby Sumatra, found the town that are strategic to do some trading activities which is Bandar Hilir. At that time, Bandar Hilir was just the simple fishing village and there are no the buildings in the red design and others. It is because there are still don’t have the colony issues happen. In this time also, it is still in the era of Malay Sultanate of Melaka. In 1511, there are some colony issues happened which the Portuguese started attacked Malacca because Malacca have the world’s leading spice trading port especially Bandar Hilir. y this time also, in 1511, Malacca's popularity was diminished because of the portuguise's rules.

Then, in 1641, Dutch started to govern and confiscate from the portuguise. The design of Bandar Hilir also started change into red and various churchs are exist. In 1641 also, Malacca's reputation and popularity started to increase from time to time within in 150 years of dutch's hand. After the dutch, British as the third country that includes in colony of Malacca until the independence of Malaysia in 1957 started to be announced by Tunku Abdul Rahman. in 1984, Bandar Hilir was filled by the various museums such asPalace Museum, Malay Islamic World Museum (DMDI), People's Museum, History Museum and UMNO Museum in area of Bandar Hilir and also followed by the Heritage Museum of Baba Nyonya and many more. Finally, in 2008, Bandar Hilir was recognized as World Heritage Sites by the United Nation Educational Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO).









































History of Communities

Within the time in 14th Century, the communities that lived there were the chinese, Malays and Indians. The major community is Malay, because of the Sultanate of Malacca era. Then, it comes with the chinese and indians as the trade workers. In 1405, the Chinese peranakan which is Baba Nyonya peoples were emerged and existed because of the Chinese groups married to local Malays. In 1511 when the Portuguese attacked Malacca, the rules of their government are so meant because they started to cultivate the Catholics between the original communities. This is another history of communities whereby the “Serani” cultural and group started emerged, that mostly Catholic groups must marry to the Indian girls according to the rules of Portuguese.
