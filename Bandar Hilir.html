Best Places in Bandar Hilir

A Famosa

It was a Portuguese fortress built in Malacca, Malaysia, in 1512. The oldest part of the fortress was a five-storey keep which gave its name to the fortress. Sometime following the Battle of Malacca (1641) and the occupation of the city by the Dutch, the keep was destroyed but the outer walls of the fortress were reinforced. However, in 1807 the British destroyed most of the fortress. The Porta de Santiago gateway, and the restored Middelburg Bastion, are the only parts of the fortress that remain today. They are among the oldest European architectural remains in Southeast Asia and the Far East.

Dataran Pahlawan Melaka

It is a shopping mall located in Banda Hilir, Malacca City, Malacca, Malaysia. It is one of the properties owned by Hatten Group Sdn Bhd, alongside nearby Hatten Square, Terminal Pahlawan, Hatten and Estadia Hotels and Hatten City Commercial Area. The mall is the biggest lifestyle shopping megamall in the state, being built on a 7.7 hectares site and have a gross area of 1.5 million sq ft and a net retail area of 750,000 sq ft. The mall building houses international fashion brands, international and local Food and beverage favourites, entertainment centres, movie cinema and karaoke.

Taming Sari Tower

The tower is named Taming Sari which is taken from the name of Hang Tuah's keris. The tower was opened to the public on 18 April 2008 and was inaugurated by the Chief Minister of Melaka, Mohd Ali Rustam on 17 May 2008. The height of the tower is 110 meters. The carriage capacity is 80 passengers and the journey takes 7 minutes. The tower can be rotated 360 degrees to see the panorama of Bandar Melaka as a whole.

The Upside Down House Melaka

It is a unique exhibition space within Malacca City, where visitors can step into an inverted version of a modern Malaysian house. Upside Down House Melaka is set along Jalan Plaza Mahkota, just a five-minute walk away from Menara Taming Sari Tower. The entrance fee for foreign visitors is RM20 for adults and RM16 for children and senior citizens. For locals (with MyKad identifications), tickets are priced at RM15 and RM10 respectively.

The Melaka River Cruise

It is a great way to see the sights of Melaka at a leisurely pace, in comfort and without breaking the bank. The Melaka River is where the town began and it has played a key role in Melaka's colourful history. Taking a river cruise is like seeing Melaka from the back door. In recent years it has had a major facelift. There has been a lot of new development but at the same time an effort has been made to preserve many of the historic buildings and bridges along the river. Even the newer buildings have been painted with colourful murals depicting different aspects of Malacca's rich history and culture.

The stadthuys

It is a historical structure situated in the heart of Malacca City, the administrative capital of the state of Malacca, Malaysia in a place known as the Red Square. The Stadhuys is known for its red exterior and nearby red clocktower. It was built by the Dutch in 1650 as the office of the Dutch Governor and Deputy Governor. Situated at Laksamana Road, beside the Christ Church, the supposed oldest remaining Dutch historical building in the Orient, is now home to the History and Ethnography Museum. Among the displays in the museum are traditional costumes and artifacts throughout the history of Malacca, which makes it Malacca's premier museum.

Best Foods in Bandar Hilir

Asam Pedas

Asam Pedas dishes are synonymous with the state of Melaka. To taste the authenticity of this cuisine, we suggest you to Asam Pedas Selera Kampung. The restaurant offers fresh fish for Asam Pedas. The various types of Asam Pedas that you can choose from include jenahak fish heads, stingrays, cencaru, and mackerel. Apart from that, other side dishes such as lobster, fried chicken squid and various sugars are also available here.

Hours of operation: 7 am - 6 pm.

Address: Jalan PM3, Mahkota Square, Bandar Hilir, 75000 Melaka.

Cendol Kampung Hulu

Unlike regular cendol, this Cendol Kampung Hulu not only promises infinite delicacy, but the presentation of this dish is also quite interesting. Usually we usually enjoy glutinous rice cendol and durian cendol only. However, if you go to Cendol Kampung Hulu, you will be served with various types of 'toppings' for the cendol. This restaurant faces the Melaka River, so you can taste the famous cendol in the city of Melaka while enjoying the beautiful view of the river.

Hours of operation: Daily, 12 noon - 12 midnight.

Address: No 26, Jalan Kampung Hulu, Kampung Hulu, 75200 Melaka.

Bebola Nasi Ayam

These chicken rice balls are among the foods you should try at the Jonker Street night market. These solid rice balls are made using flavoured rice and spicy chicken soup, giving it a more unique look in terms of texture and flavour. This delicious dish is served with tender chicken with a delicate sauce and often a mixture of homemade sauce, red chillies, garlic, and lime. Dip your chicken rice balls into this spicy sauce for a real feast in the mouth.

Address: 275, Jalan Melaka Raya 3, Taman Melaka Raya, 75000 Melaka.

Nyonya Asam Laksa

The famous Nyonya Asam Laksa in Melaka is cooked with flavor -rich cakes, coconut milk, large -sized prawns, fish balls and tau pok (fried tofu) which are often served with finely chopped cucumbers. This flavor ingredient that comes with its own flavor is a perfect example of the multicultural society in Melaka and the influence they have on the cuisine. Either way, sipping a bowl of this delicious noodle makes you want to repeatedly, even when the weather is hot.

Address: 88, Jalan Hang Jebat, 75200 Melaka

Nyonya Popiah

Hailing from China, Nyonya Popiah is made from fresh egg wraps and the popiah stalls on Jonker Street offer a variety of delicious and tender fillings such as tofu, vegetables and seafood served alongside a sweet and spicy chili sauce. If you want halal popiah, make sure you ask the stall seller first because there are stalls that use lard on their popiah. Don't worry, you can still taste this dish at Nyonya halal restaurant here.

Satay Celup

This Satay Celup in Melaka allows you to cook meat and vegetables in a hot pot containing satay gravy. Most stalls offer fresh ingredients such as fish balls, chicken balls, shrimp, bell peppers, tofu, and squid. You can pick your own and poke these ingredients on a satay skewer, dip in the satay gravy and eat hot. The best place to enjoy this dish is at Capitol Satay Celup.

Address: 45-E, Jalan Ong Kim Wee, 75300 Malacca.

Telur Puyuh Goreng

Also just like takoyaki that originated in Japan, these fried quail eggs are among the best snacks if you’re at the market tonight. These soft quail eggs are cooked crispy and added other ingredients such as chicken sausage, Nyonya chicken curry, crab sticks, tofu, and corn. What makes this food salivate is mayonnaise, cheese, and barbecue sauce. One is not enough!

Pie Tie

Often called a ‘hat cake’, Pie Tee is another traditional Nyonya cake made by frying flour shaped into a cup and then garnishing it with a refreshing mix of seafood, vegetables, lemon, and chilli. Try this food as an appetizer before you enjoy the spicy Asam and kangkung belacan dish.

Specialities in Bandar hilir

History and Ethnography Museum

It is a museum in Malacca City, Malacca, Malaysia. It is located inside the Stadthuys building, built during the Dutch Malacca administration period in 1886. The historical section of the museum displays the history of Malacca from its establishment in 1400 until 1957, the year Malaya gained independence, while the ethnography section of the museum portrays the lifestyle and culture of various communities and sub-communities of Malacca including the Malays, the Chinese, the Indians, the Portuguese, the Baba and Nyonya, the Chetti and Chitty communities. Other exhibits include items used in traditional wedding ceremonies, kitchen utensils, musical instruments and collections of ancient porcelain ware, weapons, stamps, and ancient currencies.

Malacca Sultanate Palace Museum

It a museum located in Malacca City, Malacca, Malaysia. The building is a modern reconstruction of the palace of the Malacca Sultanate, based on the information and data obtained from the Malay Annals. It is made up of hardwood (for its structure), 'belian' wood (for its roof) and wooden pegs (instead of nails) and was constructed in 1984 and became a cultural museum which showcases the history of the region. The museum was officially opened on 17 July 1986 by Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad.

Royal Malaysian Navy Museum

It is a museum which exhibits items used by the Royal Malaysian Navy in Malacca City, Malacca, Malaysia. Exhibits included photos of admirals, medals of honor, as well as information about the various ranks in the naval command, evolution of naval uniforms, naval weapons, communication equipment, navigation symbols and training programs. The museum was formerly located at Royal Malaysian Naval Base in Lumut, Perak. It was then relocated to Malacca to take the advantage of the larger number of tourists, both local and foreign visitors. The new location of the naval museum was opened to the public on 28 October 1995.


People's Museum

It is a museum in Malacca City, Malacca, Malaysia, which records and preserves the achievements of Malacca in the development sector and officially opened on 15 April 1992. It is located at the ground floor of the former Historical City of Malacca Municipal Council building, originally constructed in the 1960s and believed to be built on top of the ruins of Dutch building, which also houses the Beauty Museum and Kite Museum at the upper floors. The museum building is divided into three parts, which focuses on the development carried out for the people's benefit and the success attained by the State of Malacca: the Sports Gallery, Community Gallery and UNESCO section. In front of the building, there is a courtyard in which the transportation area is located and was established in collaboration with Malayan Railway, Royal Malaysian Air Force and West Malaysia Department of Civil Aviation. It formed a part of Taman Bunga Merdeka (Independent Flower Park), formerly known as Corronation Park, which was built by the British in 1953 to memorialise the coronation of Queen Elizabeth II.

Maritime Museum

It is a museum about maritime activities in Malacca City, Malacca, Malaysia. It was officially opened to the public by Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad on 13 June 1994, began with phase one. The phase two of the museum is housed in the old Guthrie building and was opened by State Committee for Tourism, Culture and Environment Chairman Poh Ah Tiam on 23 May 1998. The museum main exhibits the replica of Flor do Mar with 34 metres high, 36 metres long and 8 metres wide; houses exhibits, artifacts and documents from the golden era of Malacca and shows how political control of Malacca was essential to the establishment of maritime dominance in the region and also displays the trading link of Malacca from the early time through the colonial era until independence.

Malacca Stamp Museum

It is a postal museum in Malacca City, Malacca, Malaysia. It is housed in a building that was constructed using local materials and has the shape and characteristics of western architecture. The museum building was originally used as the residence for Dutch dignitaries living in Malacca until the end of World War II after which the building was abandoned. On 19 March 1954, G.E.W Wisdom, the British Resident Commissioner in Malacca, turned the building into the Malacca State Museum, until it was moved to Stadthuys in 1982. The building was also once occupied by the Malacca Islamic Foundation and the Enforcement Unit of the Malacca Municipal Council. In 2004, the building was restored by the Department of Museum and Antiquity and handed over to the Malacca State Government. In 2007, the state government, in cooperation with Pos Malaysia, decided to set up the Postal Museum in the building.
